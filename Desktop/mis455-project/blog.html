Post 1 — Why I Chose MIS

When I first started university, I was unsure whether to go into pure computer science or a more business-oriented path. Management Information Systems felt like the best of both worlds — it gives me the technical grounding to understand how software and databases work, while also teaching me how organizations use that technology to solve real problems and make decisions.

One of the most valuable lessons I have learned is that technology on its own does not create value. It is the combination of good systems thinking, user understanding, and business context that turns a tool into something useful. MIS trains you to think that way.

Post 2 — Learning HTML for the First Time

Building my first HTML page was both exciting and slightly overwhelming. There are a lot of tags to learn, and getting the structure right takes some practice. But once the basics clicked, I could see how logical and readable well-written HTML actually is. Every element has a clear purpose.

CSS was a different story. The box model and the difference between margin and padding took time to internalize. But as soon as I saw my page transform with a few lines of styling, I was hooked. This assignment pushed me to use flexbox and grid, which feel much more powerful than just floating elements.
